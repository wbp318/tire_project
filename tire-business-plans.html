Tire Business Viability — Master Report

All three scopes · Prepared for William, East Carroll Parish, LA · June 2026

What this is: a single decision document covering whether — and where — to enter the waste-tire business, plus the Arkansas/general-waste angle you asked about. Three scopes are evaluated; each also has its own standalone printable plan (Plan A/B/C HTML files).
The headline finding: Northeast Louisiana — not Acadiana — is the real processor gap, because the one NE processor (Franklin Rubber, Winnsboro) is failing under fire-marshal and town orders. Acadiana is saturated by Colt, Inc., so the Acadiana play is collection/hauling, not processing. Arkansas is optional upside on a broken state program, and "general trash" is a much bigger, capital-heavy, hauling-entry business.

So south Louisiana is covered, NW LA has Benson, and NE LA hangs on one shaky operator — Franklin Rubber, which has had 14 fire-department responses since 2010, a State Fire Marshal cease-and-desist since Sept 23, 2023, and a Town of Winnsboro vacate order. That single fact is what creates the opportunity and warns how the business fails.

3. Scope A — Northeast Louisiana Processor (Recommended)

Model: Collect tires → shred to chips with steel/wire separation → haul tire-derived fuel (TDF) to the West Monroe paper mill (~70 mi) and sell recovered steel. Matches the 800–1,500 tires/month demand LDEQ flagged for the region.

Three revenue streams (need all three)

  1. Tip fees from generators/parishes (~$1.50–2/passenger, $8–20+/large).
  2. State subsidy — min 7.5¢/lb (~$150/ton) on LDEQ-approved end use (in-state tires only).
  3. End-product sales — TDF chips + recovered steel.

Licensing (LDEQ Standard Processor Permit)

  • App fee ~$1,563 + public notice; written subsidy agreement tied to an approved end market.
  • Financial assurance $20/ton on site (bond/LOC/trust), reviewed yearly.
  • Controlled access (20-ft gates, 100-ft buffer); pile limits 10H×20W×200L, 50-ft fire lanes; storage ≤60× daily capacity.
  • Fire plan + FD agreement; vector control; manifests; 5-yr records; monthly reports by the 12th.

Capital & break-even

Startup ~$330k lean (used equipment, contract hauling) to ~$870k comfortable. Break-even is roughly 3,000 tires/month — at 1,500/mo it's break-even-to-negative. Viability = volume: aggregate East Carroll, Madison, Tensas, Richland, Franklin, Ouachita, Morehouse.

Don't repeat Franklin Rubber: rural site you control (not in-town), strict pile limits, an FD-approved fire plan, and a confirmed buyer so tires flow through the yard, never pile up.

Decisive question for LDEQ: is Franklin Rubber's permit/operation dead enough that NE LA needs a new processor? His answer gates this whole scope. Full detail: ne-louisiana-processor-plan.html.

4. Scope B — Acadiana Collector/Hauler (Lifestyle fit / on-ramp)

Why not process in Acadiana: Colt, Inc. (Scott) runs ~3M tires/yr and is a top-20 US processor. Competing head-to-head on its home turf is the textbook bad entry. Instead, supply it.

Model: Registered waste-tire transporter — contract with dealers, shops, fleets, and parish events to collect tires on a schedule and deliver to Colt. Generally needs LDEQ transporter registration + manifesting, not the heavy processor permit/financial-assurance/fire-plan burden. Confirm the tier with LDEQ.

Capital & economics

  • Startup ~$60k lean (one used truck + boxes + insurance + registration) to ~$220k multi-truck.
  • Margin ~$0.75–1.50/tire after delivery; profitability comes from route density and adding trucks.

This is the low-capital on-ramp: same skills and accounts later feed your own NE-LA plant (Scope A) instead of Colt. Run it where you want to live. Full detail: acadiana-collector-plan.html.

5. Scope C — Arkansas Tires & General Waste (Optional upside)

Arkansas tires

  • State-run via regional Used Tire Programs; no per-pound processor subsidy. ~$2.31/tire reimbursement = break-even.
  • Program ran out of money in 2022; a UTP sought $2.90→$5.01/tire in June 2025 to keep a processor; privatization debated for 2025.
  • Play it as optionality: use AR Delta tires (Lake Village, McGehee, Dermott) as incremental supply for a Louisiana operation (won't earn the LA subsidy — confirm), and get on ADEQ/SEARSWMD's list to hear about privatization/RFPs early.

General trash / MSW

  • Landfills/transfer stations/MRFs are $10M+, heavily permitted, and monopoly-held by national haulers — not a small-business entry.
  • The realistic entry is hauling: roll-off dumpsters, C&D, junk removal — and one fleet can carry tires + debris together.

Read: a dual-stream hauling business straddling the LA/AR line is more achievable than an MSW processing plant, and pairs with Scope B. Full detail: arkansas-opportunity-plan.html.
