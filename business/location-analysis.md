# Where to Site a Waste Tire Processor — Location Analysis

**Prepared for:** William (currently East Carroll Parish, LA) · **Date:** June 2026
**Bottom line up front:** Acadiana (your preferred place to live) is the *worst* place to start a competing processor — it's the most saturated market in the state. Northeast Louisiana is the real gap, and it just got more interesting because the one NE processor is failing. Arkansas is a separate, riskier play covered in `arkansas-opportunity.md`.

---

## The fact that changes everything: the actual LDEQ processor list

Our earlier notes said NE Louisiana's nearest processor was "~200 miles south." **That was wrong.** The official LDEQ Permitted Waste Tire Processor list (last updated Jan 17, 2025) shows **five** permitted processors, and they are *not* all in the south:

| Processor | Location | Region | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Franklin Rubber Resources LLC** | **Winnsboro (Franklin Parish)** | **NE LA — ~60–70 mi from you** | **Troubled / on-and-off** (see below) |
| Benson Environmental Services | Sibley (Webster Parish, near Minden) | NW LA — ~150 mi | Operational |
| Colt, Inc. (×2 permits) | Scott (Lafayette) + New Orleans | Acadiana + SE | Operational — dominant |
| Environmental Industries Recycling | Port Allen (Baton Rouge) | South-central — ~190 mi | Operational |
| Baum Industries, LLC | Baton Rouge | South-central | **Not currently operational** |

So the map is: south Louisiana is covered, NW LA has Benson, and **NE LA's only processor (Franklin Rubber) is the weak link.**

---

## Region 1 — Acadiana (your preferred home): saturated, do not compete

- **Colt, Inc. (Scott, LA)** processes ~3 million scrap tires/year — *by far* the state's largest, and a top-20 processor nationally. It also runs dumpsite remediation statewide.
- A new processor opening in Colt's backyard would be fighting the incumbent for the same generators on the incumbent's home turf, with worse economies of scale on day one. This is the textbook bad entry.
- **But there's still an Acadiana play that fits "live where I want":** be a **collector/transporter**, not a processor. You'd run roll-off boxes / a trailer, contract with tire dealers and parishes to haul tires, and *sell them into Colt* (or feed your own NE plant). Far lower capital (no shredder, no financial-assurance-heavy pile permit at processor scale), and you're a supplier to Colt rather than a competitor. This is the realistic way to base yourself in Acadiana.

## Region 2 — Northeast Louisiana (near home): the real gap, with a cautionary tale

**The gap is real because the incumbent is failing.** Franklin Rubber Resources in Winnsboro (~60–70 mi from East Carroll) is the only NE processor, and its track record is a warning sign *and* an opening:

- **14 fire-department responses since 2010** at its Industrial Drive tire piles.
- **State Fire Marshal cease-and-desist** since **Sept 23, 2023**; lifted only *temporarily* in early 2025 "for the sole purpose of cleanup … mitigating current hazards."
- **Town of Winnsboro ordered it to vacate** its industrial location.
- Long history of **sporadic pickups** and prior closures; "temporarily ceased operations" to evaluate "significant" upgrades.
- Its VP has publicly said the **$1.50/tire** the processor nets (after LDEQ keeps $0.50 of the $2 fee) **isn't enough to run the business.**

**What this tells you — both halves matter:**
1. **Opportunity:** If Franklin Rubber stays shut or gets forced out, NE LA generators (dealers, parishes) have *no* nearby outlet again, and the West Monroe industrial corridor is right there. A clean, well-run operator could capture that volume.
2. **Cautionary tale:** This is *exactly* how tire processing goes wrong — uncontrolled piles, fires, community backlash, regulators shutting you down, and margins too thin to fund the cleanup. Whatever you build must be the opposite: disciplined pile management, a fire plan the FD actually signs off on, and a guaranteed end market so tires move *through* the site instead of piling up.

**Why NE LA still scores best for a processor:**
- Closest to home (no relocation from East Carroll required).
- **Graphic Packaging paper mill, West Monroe (~70 mi)** is the nearest large industrial fuel user (TDF candidate) — see `end-market-buyers.md`.
- Real, demonstrated demand (the Franklin Parish "tire overload" complaints, LDEQ's West Monroe/Shreveport town-hall sessions).
- **Best siting tactic:** don't site *in* a town. Franklin Rubber's core mistake was an in-town industrial lot with neighbors. Put a controlled-access site on rural/ag land with the 100-ft buffer and fire lanes built in from day one — something you, as a farmer with land, are well positioned to do.

## Region 3 — Arkansas: separate play, see dedicated write-up

Arkansas's *statewide* tire program is government-run and **broke** (ran out of money in 2022; privatization debated for 2025). That's a different risk/reward profile — covered fully in `arkansas-opportunity.md`. Short version: a possible privatization opening, but the underlying economics are even weaker than Louisiana's, and the general-trash angle you raised is a much larger, capital-heavy, monopoly-held business.

---

## Recommendation

1. **Processor → Northeast Louisiana**, on rural land you control, sited to avoid the in-town mistakes that are sinking Franklin Rubber. This keeps you near home, near the West Monroe end market, and aimed at a gap that's actively reopening.
2. **If living in Acadiana matters more than maximizing the processor margin** → run a **collection/hauling** business there that feeds Colt. Much lower capital, no head-to-head fight, and it still earns money in tires.
3. **Before any capital outlay,** the single decisive question for Donlon at LDEQ (draft in `ldeq-outreach-email.md`) is now sharper: *"What is Franklin Rubber Resources' current status, and does LDEQ see room for an additional NE Louisiana processor?"* His answer tells you whether the gap is open.
