# Startup Budget & Revenue Model — Small "Shred-and-Haul" Processor

**Model:** Collect tires → shred to chips (with steel/wire separation) → haul TDF to a paper mill/cement kiln and steel to a scrap outlet. This is the lowest-capital path and matches the 800–1,500 tires/month gap LDEQ flagged.

> **All figures are illustrative planning ranges — confirm with equipment quotes, an insurance agent, a surety bond agent, and LDEQ before relying on them.** The internet's "$35M tire recycling plant" figure is for a full crumb-rubber/pyrolysis operation — NOT this model.

---

## A. One-time startup capital

| Item | Low (used/lean) | High (new) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary shredder (~1–2 ton/hr) | $80,000 | $250,000 | Used single-shaft entry units cheaper; new high-capacity $150–300k |
| Steel/wire separation (magnet/conveyor) | $15,000 | $80,000 | Needed if selling de-wired TDF to paper mill |
| Loader / skid steer w/ grapple | $35,000 | $70,000 | Used farm-grade may already be on hand |
| Hauling: roll-off truck + boxes (or trailer) | $50,000 | $130,000 | Or contract a transporter to cut capital |
| Site prep: gravel pad, fencing, 20-ft gates, drainage, fire lanes, water | $40,000 | $100,000 | Per LAC 33:VII.10525 site standards |
| Truck scale | $30,000 | $70,000 | Needed to weigh in/out for subsidy & tip fees |
| Permit application fee | $1,563 | $1,563 | LDEQ Standard Processor Permit |
| Permit engineering/legal/plans | $20,000 | $50,000 | Fire plan, vector plan, application prep |
| Financial assurance (bond *premium*, yr 1) | $1,000 | $5,000 | Premium ≈ 1–3% of bond face; bond face = $20/ton on site |
| Initial insurance (commercial liability) | $5,000 | $15,000 | Annual; first-year outlay |
| Working capital (3–6 mo opex) | $50,000 | $100,000 | Cash to operate before revenue stabilizes |
| **TOTAL** | **~$330,000** | **~$870,000** | Leanest realistic start ~$300k; comfortable ~$600–900k |

## B. Monthly revenue (illustrative, at two volumes)

Assumes ~22 lb per passenger-tire-equivalent (PTE). Subsidy = 7.5¢/lb = **$1.65/PTE (~$150/ton)**.

| Stream | At 1,500 tires/mo (~16.5 t) | At 3,000 tires/mo (~33 t) |
|---|---|---|
| Tip fees in (blended ~$2.50/tire *(confirm)*) | $3,750 | $7,500 |
| State subsidy (7.5¢/lb on approved end use) | $2,475 | $4,950 |
| TDF + steel sales, net of freight | $0–1,500 | $0–3,000 |
| **Gross monthly revenue** | **~$6,200–7,700** | **~$12,500–15,500** |

## C. Monthly operating cost (illustrative)

| Item | Range |
|---|---|
| Labor (1–2 workers) | $4,000–8,000 |
| Fuel + equipment maintenance | $1,500–3,500 |
| Freight to end-buyer (if not in tip/sales above) | $1,000–3,000 |
| Insurance, bond, utilities, reporting/admin | $1,500–3,000 |
| **Total monthly opex** | **~$8,000–17,500** |

## D. The honest read
- At **1,500 tires/mo**, the operation is roughly **break-even to slightly negative** — too small to carry the fixed costs alone.
- At **~3,000+ tires/mo**, it moves toward **modest profit**, and the math keeps improving with volume because most costs are fixed.
- **Conclusion: viability = volume.** You must aggregate tires from *multiple* NE parishes (East Carroll, Madison, Tensas, Richland, Morehouse, Ouachita, etc.) and keep the haul to your end-buyer short. Being the region's *only* processor is what lets you both set tip fees and capture that volume.

## E. Levers that most change the outcome
1. **Volume** — secure parish/dealer supply contracts *before* launch (LDEQ town hall says parishes want a local option).
2. **End-buyer freight** — the closer your approved buyer (Graphic Packaging at ~70 mi helps a lot), the better the margin.
3. **Capital discipline** — buying used equipment and contracting the hauling (vs. owning a truck) can roughly halve startup capital.
4. **Grants** — ask LDEQ/EPA about recycling grants; the town hall report repeatedly raised grant funding for tire recycling and parish programs.
