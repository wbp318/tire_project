# Arkansas Opportunity — Tire Recycling & General Waste

**Prepared for:** William · **Date:** June 2026
**Why this file exists:** You flagged that Arkansas "lacks trash processors," and East Carroll Parish sits right on the AR line (Lake Village ~12 mi). This evaluates two separate Arkansas plays: (A) waste **tires**, and (B) **general trash/MSW**. They have very different economics.

---

## A. Arkansas waste tires — a broken state program (opportunity + big caveat)

### How Arkansas runs tires (very different from Louisiana)
- Arkansas does **not** pay private processors a per-pound subsidy the way Louisiana does. Instead it runs the **Tire Accountability Program (TAP)** through **regional "Used Tire Programs" (UTPs)** operated by the regional Solid Waste Management Districts. Funding comes from a **$3 rim/tire fee**; after deductions, only about **$2.31/tire** is left to reimburse the UTPs — described as the *break-even* point, not profit.
- The district contracts haulers/processors. Example near you: the **Southeast Arkansas Regional Solid Waste Management District (SEARSWMD, "myseagreen")** runs free drop-off (4 tires/person/month, then $2.90/tire) and contracts hauling ("Get Rid of It"). Collection centers across the Delta: DeWitt, Warren, Rison, Tillar-Winchester, Sheridan, White Hall.

### The problem = the opportunity
- The program **ran out of money in August 2022**; tires piled up. The Legislature has repeatedly declined to raise the fee, so **revenue doesn't cover rising transport/processing costs.**
- One UTP's contractor became "operationally inconsistent"; in **June 2025** a UTP asked to raise its reimbursement rate from **$2.90 → $5.01/tire** just to keep a processor willing to operate.
- The program's own architect (Rep. Lanny Fite) has pushed to **dismantle and privatize** it; privatization legislation was expected to be taken up in **2025**.

**Read:** Arkansas is a state where the public tire system is failing and may hand the job to the private market. If privatization happens, a private processor/hauler could step in to contracts that are currently starved. **But** the reason it's failing is structural — *there isn't enough money in the fee to make processing profitable.* Privatization doesn't fix that; it just moves the risk to you. Officials themselves warned privatization means "less service and more expense."

### How to play it (low-risk, optionality)
- **Don't build a plant for the Arkansas program.** Treat AR as *incremental supply* for a Louisiana-based operation: AR Delta generators near the line (Lake Village, McGehee, Dermott) could feed a NE-LA processor, subject to LDEQ's in-state-tire rules for the *subsidy* (AR tires likely won't earn the LA subsidy — confirm with Donlon; they'd be tip-fee/end-market revenue only).
- **Watch the 2025–26 privatization bills.** If Arkansas opens processor contracts to the private market, that's the trigger to re-evaluate — get on the contact list with **ADEQ's Tire Accountability Program** and **SEARSWMD** now so you hear about RFPs/contracts early.

## B. General trash / MSW — bigger money, but a different (capital-heavy) business

Honest assessment, because you asked: "trash processing" is **not** a close cousin of tire processing.

- **Landfills, transfer stations, and material-recovery facilities (MRFs)** are capital-intensive (often $10M+), heavily permitted, long lead-time, and in most regions effectively controlled by **national haulers (Waste Connections, Republic, WM)** and the regional Solid Waste Management Districts that hold the permits. This is a monopoly/utility-style business, not a small-business entry.
- The realistic small-business entry into "trash" is **collection/hauling**, not processing: residential/commercial **roll-off dumpsters, construction debris (C&D) hauling, junk removal**. Evidence the Arkansas market has room: new entrants like "Dirty South Dumpsters" expanding in central Arkansas (Sept 2025). This needs trucks + boxes, not a permitted processing facility.
- **Where tires and trash overlap usefully for you:** a **roll-off / hauling business** can carry *both* scrap tires *and* C&D/junk on the same trucks and boxes, smoothing revenue. That's the practical synergy — one fleet, multiple waste streams — rather than trying to be an MSW *processor.*

**Read:** If the goal is "a money opportunity near home with manageable capital," a **dual-stream hauling business (tires + roll-off debris)** straddling the LA/AR line is more achievable than an MSW processing facility, and it pairs naturally with the tire-collector option in `location-analysis.md`.
