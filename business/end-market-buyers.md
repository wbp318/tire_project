# End-Market Buyers Within Range of Lake Providence, LA

You earn the state subsidy **only** on material that reaches an **LDEQ-approved beneficial end use**, and your product revenue depends on a real buyer. This is the list to work before buying equipment. **All distances are approximate and all contacts must be confirmed by call.** Get LDEQ to confirm each as an *approved* end use before counting on it.

> Reminder from research: **Drax (Bastrop / Morehouse Parish) makes wood pellets and does NOT burn tire-derived fuel — it is not a buyer.** Don't let its closeness mislead you.

---

## Tier 1 — Primary target (closest large fuel user)

### Graphic Packaging International — West Monroe, LA  ·  ~70 mi
- Major paperboard mill with biomass/boiler operations; one of the region's largest industrial fuel users.
- **Fit:** Tire-derived fuel (TDF) as a supplement to wood waste. **Caveat:** paper-mill boilers generally need **de-wired chips** (steel clogs feed systems) — this affects your equipment choice (you'd need wire separation).
- **Action:** Ask for the mill's fuel/purchasing or environmental manager; ask whether they currently accept or would pilot TDF, and to what spec (chip size, wire content, moisture).
- Contact: West Monroe mill — *(confirm direct line; general info via Monroe Chamber listing)*

---

## Tier 2 — Cement kilns (take whole tires + wire, but farther)

### Ash Grove Cement — Foreman, AR  ·  long haul, ~200+ mi *(confirm)*
- Cement kiln in SW Arkansas with a **historical tire-derived-fuel air permit**. Cement kilns are the ideal TDF user because they consume **both the rubber and the steel** (no de-wiring needed).
- **Caveat:** distance is significant from East Carroll Parish — freight may kill the economics unless you ship full loads infrequently. Worth a call to gauge interest and price.
- **Action:** Ask whether they're currently sourcing TDF and at what delivered price/spec.

*(Also worth a search: any closer cement/lime kiln in MS or AR. No Louisiana cement kiln was found in range — LA has terminals, not manufacturing kilns.)*

---

## Tier 3 — Recovered steel outlet (for bead/belt wire you separate)

### Nucor Steel Arkansas — Blytheville, AR  ·  ~150 mi *(confirm)*
- 7301 E County Road 142, Blytheville, AR 72315. Largest steel recycler in North America; scrap brokered through The David J. Joseph Company.
- **Fit:** sells your recovered tire steel as ferrous scrap (a secondary revenue line, not your main one).
- **Action:** Ask David J. Joseph / Nucor scrap purchasing about specs and pricing for tire-derived steel. *(confirm there isn't a closer scrap yard in Monroe — likely cheaper to sell wire to a local Monroe-area scrap dealer than haul to Blytheville.)*

---

## Other end uses to ask LDEQ about (no equipment-heavy processing of fuel)
- **Civil-engineering / agricultural uses** of tire chips (septic/leachate drainage, road sub-base) — sometimes approvable with less refinement.
- **Crumb rubber** (playground/athletic surfacing, rubberized asphalt) — higher value but needs granulation equipment (mid-scale model).
- **Pyrolysis** — capital-intensive; not a starter.

---

## How to use this list
1. Confirm with LDEQ (Donlon) which of these are *approved* end uses from NE LA.
2. Get at least **one letter of intent** from a buyer (ideally Graphic Packaging) — this de-risks the whole plan and strengthens your permit application.
3. Let the chosen buyer's **spec** (de-wired vs. whole, chip size) drive your equipment purchase — not the other way around.
