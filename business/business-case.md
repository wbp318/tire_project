# Northeast Louisiana Waste Tire Processing — One-Page Business Case

**Prepared for:** William (East Carroll Parish, LA) · **Date:** June 2026
**Status:** Draft for decision — figures marked *(confirm)* need a phone call or quote to finalize.

---

## The opportunity in one sentence
Northeast Louisiana has **no waste tire processor**, every NE parish currently long-hauls tires ~200 miles south (or they get dumped illegally), and the state both *pays a per-pound subsidy* for processing and has a *statutory mandate to grow in-state recycling businesses* — so a local processor fills a real, state-backed gap.

## Why the gap is real
- Per LDEQ's official list (Jan 17, 2025) Louisiana has **five permitted processors**, but only **one in the northeast — Franklin Rubber Resources in Winnsboro (~60–70 mi away) — and it is failing** (fire-marshal cease-and-desist since Sept 2023, town vacate order, repeated closures). The rest are concentrated south (Colt in Scott/Lafayette + New Orleans, Environmental Industries in Port Allen) plus Benson in Sibley (NW LA); Baum in Baton Rouge is not operational. So NE LA's effective coverage hangs on one shaky operator. **See `location-analysis.md` for the full map and the decision.**
- LDEQ's own 2024 Waste Tire Town Hall held sessions in **West Monroe and Shreveport** specifically because the north end of the state is underserved, and flagged demand for operations processing **800–1,500 tires/month**. It also launched a 2025 **Waste Tire Pilot Program** (parish/municipal cleanup reimbursements, runs through June 30, 2026) — a signal of how much illegal-dump pressure exists, and a potential customer relationship, not a processor payment.
- Governing statute **La. R.S. 30:2411(B)** states a purpose to "encourage the development of the state's recycling industry" and to "look favorably on Louisiana businesses in the recycling industry."

## The three revenue streams (you need all three)
1. **Tip fees** charged to generators/parishes — parishes currently pay ~$1.50–2.00 per passenger tire, $8–20+ for large/ag *(confirm)*.
2. **State subsidy** — minimum **7.5¢/lb (~$150/ton)** for material reaching an LDEQ-approved end-market use (LAC 33:VII.10535). Note: the rule says this is a *supplement*, not full-cost coverage.
3. **End-product sales** — tire-derived fuel (TDF) and recovered steel.

> The single make-or-break factor: **you only earn the subsidy when material reaches an LDEQ-APPROVED end market.** Securing a buyer comes *before* buying equipment.

## Nearest realistic end markets (see `end-market-buyers.md`)
- **Graphic Packaging paper mill, West Monroe (~70 mi)** — closest large industrial boiler; TDF candidate (requires de-wired chips). *Primary target.*
- **Ash Grove Cement, Foreman, AR** — cement kiln with a historical TDF permit; takes whole tires + wire, but a long haul. *(confirm distance/interest)*
- **Nucor Steel, Blytheville, AR** — outlet for recovered bead/belt steel. *(confirm)*

## What it takes (see `startup-budget.md` for numbers)
- **Standard Waste Tire Processor Permit** (app fee ~$1,563) + public-notice process
- **Financial assurance** = $20/ton of tire material on site (bond/LOC/trust, reviewed yearly)
- Controlled-access site, fire-protection & vector-control plans, manifests, monthly reports, 5-yr records

## The honest bottom line
Per-tire margins are thin; **this is a throughput + logistics business.** It works because (a) you'd be the *only* processor in the region, capturing all NE volume and setting tip fees, and (b) you cut the freight that southern processors can't avoid. Viability hinges on aggregating multiple parishes' volume and keeping the haul to your end-buyer short.
