1. The one rule that drives everything

Whole scrap tires are banned from Louisiana landfills. They must go to a registered tire dealer, a permitted waste-tire processor, or an authorized parish collection point — so a landfill is usually not the answer for whole tires.

Louisiana runs a Waste Tire Program through the Dept. of Environmental Quality (LDEQ), funded by a fee you already paid when buying tires (roughly $2.25 passenger/light-truck, ~$5 medium/commercial truck, ~$10 off-road/large per tire — confirm current amounts). Because that fee is prepaid, many dealers take back scrap tires (free or small fee) and processors are paid by the state to recycle them.

Upshot: your cheapest legal path is almost always a tire dealer or a parish collection point — not a landfill.

2. Tier 1 — the easy ~30 passenger & farm-truck tires

  1. East Carroll Parish Police Jury / solid-waste office (Lake Providence). First call. Ask whether the parish runs a scrap-tire collection day or designated drop-off — parishes get state grants for periodic tire amnesty events that are often FREE to residents.
  2. Local tire / auto shops (Lake Providence, Tallulah ~25 mi, Monroe ~70 mi). Ask: "Do you take scrap tires for disposal, and what's the per-tire fee?" Typically $2–4 per passenger tire.
  3. Walmart Auto Care Center — nearest in Tallulah, LA and Lake Village, AR (~12 mi). Many take old tires (~$1.50–3 each) even if not bought there — store-dependent, so call that store first.

3. Tier 2 — the tractor/ag tires (the costly, tricky part)

Big ag tires are a different problem: most shops, Walmart, and even some landfills won't take them, or charge by weight. Budget $15–60+ each.

Where they can go — call ahead before driving:

  • The dealer that sells tractor tires (Lake Providence / Tallulah / Monroe) — takes old casings, sometimes free with a new-tire purchase.
  • A permitted waste-tire processor that handles OTR/ag tires — most likely in the Monroe area or an extended hub (Section 5). Ask LDEQ (Section 6).
  • The parish/regional landfill — ask: "Do you accept large ag tires, and is it per tire or by weight?"

Money-savers: cut them in halves/quarters with a reciprocating saw (less volume, sometimes lower fee); remove rims to avoid tire-on-rim surcharges; sort by type so you only pay ag rates on the ag tires.

4. Free / get-paid angles (be realistic)

The honest truth: scrap tires almost always cost money to offload; getting paid is rare for a mixed junk pile. Aim to avoid cost, not earn. (The one "cash" idea — cutting truck-tire sidewalls to sell as silage rings to outfits like 24tons/KSI Supply — isn't worth the labor for one ~40-tire pile, and they don't buy whole tires from the public.) The cheapest "disposal" is free reuse, and your area is ideal: ~74% of East Carroll Parish is farmland, so the cattle/hay crowd that uses these tires is right next door. Best plays, in order:

  • Tractor-tire water troughs & feed bunks — the best home for your big tractor tires. One large tire becomes a near-indestructible cattle water trough (cut one sidewall, seal the bottom) or feed bunk. Ranchers seek these out — let them do the cutting.
  • Silage / hay-tarp weights — whole car/truck tires are the classic weight over plastic on silage piles, baleage, and hay tarps; one producer can absorb dozens. ⚠️ Climate caveat: whole tires hold water and breed mosquitoes (West Nile), and LA limits on-farm stockpiles — pitch them for active use laid flat on tarps, not stacked storage.
  • Raised garden beds / retaining & erosion walls — soaks up odd large tires the cattle crowd skips; gardeners take smaller passenger tires for planters.

How to reach the demand:

  1. Post two separate free-pickup ads on Facebook Marketplace (set location to Monroe to cover NE LA): one "Free used tires — you haul" for passenger/farm-truck tires, and a separate "Free tractor & ag tires — great for silage weights, troughs, feed bunks" so the right buyer self-selects. Bump every few days; allow ~2 weeks for the biggest tires.
  2. Local parish buy/sell/trade & farm groups — search Facebook for Parish Buy Sell Trade, Tallulah/Lake Providence yard sale, Delta Cattlemen, NELA farm equipment.
  3. LSU AgCenter — East Carroll / Madison Parish Extension office. The parish livestock/ag agent knows local producers and can connect you to someone who wants tarp-weight or trough tires. Main line 225-578-4161 (HQ switchboard — ask for your parish office), or use "Find Your Agent" at lsuagcenter.com.

  2. LDEQ Waste Tire Program — single point of contact 1-866-896-5337 (confirm). Ask for the nearest permitted processor that takes both passenger AND large ag tires.
  3. Nearest tractor-tire dealer (Lake Providence / Tallulah / Monroe) — will they take your old ag tires, and the per-tire fee?

8. Do / Don't

Do

  • Call ahead every time — confirm tire types accepted and the fee.
  • Sort tires by type before loading.
  • Keep disposal receipts (proof of legal disposal).
  • Post the tractor tires "free, you haul" before paying to dump them.

Don't

  • Don't dump or burn them — steep LA penalties, plus mosquito and fire hazards.
  • Don't haul big ag tires anywhere without calling first — a wasted trip with a loaded trailer is the most common mistake.
